# Privacy Policy for Daylight (Turn Off Dark Mode)

_Last updated: 6 July 2026_

Daylight does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data.

- The extension saves only your auto-mode setting, your per-site on/off preferences, and the chosen method. This is stored using Chrome's built-in `storage.sync`, which keeps it inside your own Google/Chrome account. The developer has no access to it.
- The extension makes no network requests. It contains no analytics, tracking, advertising, or third-party code.
- The extension does not read, collect, or send the content of the pages you visit. It only injects a stylesheet to change how a page looks in your own browser.

Because no data is collected, there is nothing to request, export, or delete beyond removing the extension, which clears its stored preferences.
